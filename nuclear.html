Binding energy is the energy required to break a nucleus into free nucleons. Mass defect Δm gives BE via E=Δm c². The semi‑empirical mass formula (Weizsäcker) approximates BE(A,Z) and explains the BE/A peak near iron.
SEMF estimate & readouts
Semi‑empirical mass formula (one common parameter set):
  BE(A,Z) = a_v A − a_s A^{2/3} − a_c Z(Z−1)/A^{1/3} − a_a (A−2Z)²/A ± a_p / A^{1/2}
Where coefficients (MeV): a_v≈15.8, a_s≈18.3, a_c≈0.714, a_a≈23.2, a_p≈12 (pairing term + for even–even, − for odd–odd, 0 otherwise).
BE per nucleon peaks around A≈56–62 (iron/nickel). Fusion of light nuclei and fission of heavy nuclei both move toward higher BE/A.
